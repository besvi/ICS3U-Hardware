Graphic Tablet

Overview

A graphic tablet is a device used to allow drawing or tracing via a stylus or pen rather than with a mouse
Unlike with a traditional mouse, the use of a stylus and tablet allow for precision tracking relative to the stylus' position in space relative to the trackpad. A mouse bases the cursor's position by the physical movement, regardless of where the mouse is in space.

Quick History

Telautograph (1888) - Elisha Gray
Acoustic tablets
The first graphic tablet was an electronic handwriting device patented by Elisha Gray in the year 1888. Gray Named the apparatus the Telautograph.

Uses

  • handwriting, signatures, tracings
  • widely considered to offer a very natural way to create computer graphics
Graphic tablets were originally developed as a device to allow for handwriting electronically, as well as for handwriting recognition by computer software. Lower grade tablets can be found for relatively affordable prices, and are typically utilized by hobbyist beginner artists, as the dedicated monetary investment is not so steep.

Components and Connections

USB 2.0 port, HDMI, Power

Price Range

Under $500 USD - over $2,000 USD
